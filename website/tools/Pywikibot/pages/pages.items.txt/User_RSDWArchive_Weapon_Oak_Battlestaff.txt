{{Journal entry|
Saradominist mages preferred the hefty swing of an oak staff over the more pliable ash. They felt that sturdier oak allowed for more powerful spells. The more durable wood was less prone to shattering itself into splinters when they cast their most devastating magic. 

While most wizards practised familiar spells, pushing their research into different practical applications for what was already known, one wizard would push the boundaries of spellcasting. Her name was Cynthia Valtis, and she would go down in local legend.

She would have liked to be remembered for her ability to transform twigs into carrots, thus preventing the famine that threatened Brynmoor. She would have wanted to be remembered for her clever application of wind and water magic to create a nice cool breeze during the summer heat. But, alas, she would be remembered for an ill-advised fire magic experiment that would see her travel across the continent faster than any wizard before. 

Her body has yet to be found.
